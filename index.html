About

I am a first-year Computer Science PhD student at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign where I work on physics-informed AI for next-generation wireless systems. My research combines radio propagation theory with modern machine learning to build reliable channel models, ultra-massive MIMO beamformers, and reconfigurable metasurface controllers for 6G networks.

Previously, I completed an M.Sc. (Thesis) in Electrical Engineering at The University of Texas at Dallas focusing on RF learning, and a B.Sc. in Electrical & Electronic Engineering at BUET. I am broadly excited about trustworthy wireless digital twins, sensing-informed communication, and the intersection of electromagnetics with deep learning.

Projects

  • Latency–Weather Analysis on ARA: Empirical study of weather impacts on wireless KPIs with predictive modeling on rural testbeds.
  • IOSVB: Interference-Optimized Singular Vector Beamforming: Low-complexity MU-MIMO beamforming with provable interference control.
  • Neural RF Fields: Neural radiance field adaptations for multi-frequency channel estimation and propagation modeling.
